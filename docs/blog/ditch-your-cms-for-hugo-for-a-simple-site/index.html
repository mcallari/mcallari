Ditch Your CMS by Using Hugo and GitHub Pages to Deploy a Truly Static Site

By Michael Callari on October 2, 2018

For developers, product managers, and directors we are continuously striving to find a development, user experience, and cost equilibrium for our projects. Each person on the team wants to complete projects efficiently without sacrificing quality.

In my career as a CMS developer, project manager, and solution architect, one of the most frustrating things is being forced to use the wrong tool for the job at hand because of a reason outside the control of the project team. The most common example of this is having to deploy a full-scale CMS to produce a simple, informational site with a blog or blog-like components.

In building out the three-x-square site, Laune and I wanted to explore ditching the CMS and give Hugo a try. I will walk you through the main components of a basic site and how Hugo got us up and running to see if this approach will be right for your next project.

The Tech Needs

With any new approach there will likey be trade-offs, and those have to be measured against the business goals at hand to determine if the project is viable. We knew the following features were required and had to work without us breaking any patterns Hugo had set up:
